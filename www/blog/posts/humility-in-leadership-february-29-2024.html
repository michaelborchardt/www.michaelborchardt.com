February 29th, 2024 - Humility in Leadership.

While I perceive being humble as a noble trait, I understand this trait as very marketable when found in people who are in leadership roles. Foremost, I should define leadership. Leadership is (1) being responsible for other humans, and (2) caring about other humans wellness. I do not believe being responsible for other people is enough to qualify a person as a leader. However, when a person is responsible for other people and is caring about the wellness of these individuals, I believe this is drilling into what leadership is.

Furthermore, humility manifests itself in many forms. For example, I understand humility as being willing to work with the people below you in the chain of command. When a subordinate approaches a boss with an idea, issue, or comment, then the boss should provide a listening ear. This is humility.

However, when in a leadership role, having humility does not equate to doing everything for a subordinate. In my own opinion, being a humble leader is taking the time to work with a subordinate. Teaching a human to do something for themselves is much more effective than doing something for them. While a good leader does accomplish goals, I believe a good leader does this with the help of the individuals they lead. They do not do this all on their own.

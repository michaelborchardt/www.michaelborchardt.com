What is a Variable?

A variable is a placeholder for a numerical value. In mathematics, a variable may take the place of a number of values. A variable is often denoted with the characters (y) or (x). In statistics, a variable may be either quantitative or qualitative. A qualitative variable is something that is observed and recorded. For example, listing the colors of a series of cars that drive by would be defining qualitative variables. A quantitative variable is a numerical value. For example, the amount of money in your bank account, your grade point average, or the number of bedrooms in your house are all examples of quantitative variables. Quantitative variables can be either discrete or continuous. Discrete variables can assume only certain values, and they're gaps between the values. Observations of a continuous variable can assume any value within a specific range.

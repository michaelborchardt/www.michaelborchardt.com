Title: How to Perform a Threat Analysis?

A threat analysis begins with taking inventory of an organization's assets. Furthermore, a threat analysis involves assessing the types of risk associated with each asset. An asset is something of value that could be perceived as useful to the business, or something that is vital to business operations. Assets could be computers and other hardware, as well as algorithms and other proprietary information. After we've defined the risk associated with each asset, we can start to define groups of actors who may want to exploit such risks. Moreover, people who want to exploit risks are considered threats. Risks that are capable of being exploited are defined as vulnerabilities.

As we've come to understand, vulnerabilties are risks that are capable of being exploited. A threat is a person who is capable of exploiting a vulnerability. If your goal is to eliminate all risk associated with operating a business or organization, you're going to have a bad time. The evolution of technology is constant, and risk landscapes are ever evolving. Although the idea to eliminate all risk is sound, the practical application of this concept is inconceivable. What we can do is eliminate as much risk as possible, and we can also remain constantly vigiliant of risks evolving on the horizon.

A good threat analysis begins with a comprehensive list of assets, a detailed report of risk associated with each asset, an analysis of how these risks can be exploited, a report of who is able to exploit these vulnerabilities, and an understanding that not all risks will be able to be mitigated. As a business owner, it's your job to decide which risks are worth the expense to mitigate.
